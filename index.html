SpiceJet Airlines

SpiceJet is known as India’s low-cost airline and the sixth-largest airline in carrying passengers. It operates over 600 flights daily to 60 domestic routes and 13 International. It is one of the most opted airlines, offering special fares and efficient operations.

Spice Jet serves India’s prominent cities such as Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Jaipur, Chandigarh, and Kochi, as well as some key international destinations such as Bangkok & Dubai. Spice Jet’s fleet comprises Boeing 737s & Bombardier Q400S, approximately 90 in count.

About SpiceJet Airlines

When booking SpiceJet flights online, the information below will be helpful.
SpiceJet, India’s most affordable air travel service provider, was established in 2004. Its headquarters are in Gurgaon, Haryana, and it has captured approximately 4% of the market share. SpiceJet’s first commercial flight took place on May 24, 2005 (Delhi to Mumbai) with its first aircraft, a Boeing 737-800.
Gradually, SpiceJet became a prominent player in the Indian aviation hub, offering affordable air travel services. In 2008, Spice Jet became the third largest low-cost carrier airline after Air Decann & Indigo; it has consistently expanded it’s fleet and services since then.

How to book SpiceJet ticket online?

To book a SpiceJet flight online, you can:

  • Go to spicejet.com
  • Check for flight availability and fares
  • Select a flight and fares
  • Agree to the terms and conditions
  • Fill in your details
  • Complete the payment process
  • Generate your itinerary

Option-02: You can also book a SpiceJet flight by:

  • Calling SpiceJet's 24-hour reservations at +91 (0)124 4983410 or +91 (0)124 7101600.
  • Visiting a SpiceJet walk-in airport ticket counter or sales office
  • Booking through a travel agent Nixtour.

After booking, you'll receive an e-ticket and passenger name record (PNR) by email. You can print your e-ticket at any SpiceJet ticketing counter in India, but there's a charge of INR 100 for each e-ticket, except for business class seats.

You can web check in for SpiceJet flights between 48 hours and 2 hours before your flight's departure. For some flights, you can web check in up to 54 hours before departure.

How to Check the Flight Status of a SpiceJet flight?

To check the arrival and departure or for any information related to delay or cancellation, you need to should check the flight status. In order to do it, follow the procedures mentioned below:

  • Enter the city you will arrive at.
  • Fill in the date.
  • Enter the flight number.
  • Click on the Search button to know your flight status.

SpiceJet Airlines Fleet

  • SpiceJet’s fleet size is 97, comprising a mix of Boeing and Bombardier aircrafts.
  • These aircraft are designed to support extensive domestic and international operations.
  • The Boeing 737s are pillars of the Spice jet, catering to mid haul and long haul routes; on the other hand, Bombardiers are known for short-haul or regional routes.
  • As of September 2024, Spice Jet operates over 90 flights with a fleet comprising Boeing 737-700, 737-800, 738-900, 737 MAX & De Havilland Canada Dash 8-400. Where Boing planes offer 126 – 220 seats, ideal for long haul routes, Bombardier has 74 – 99 seats optimized for quicker take-offs and landing.

SpiceJet operates on a point-to-point basis and is not responsible for any delays experienced with either SpiceJet or other carriers. However, an exception applies to SpiceJet’s connecting flights that are specifically offered as "connections" in the system.

For passengers connecting from a SpiceJet domestic flight to a SpiceJet international flight or vice versa, the international flight's Free Baggage Allowance will apply for domestic flights within 24 hours of landing in India.

For those traveling on a SpiceJet domestic flight with a connection on another airline to or from an international destination, each passenger is allowed one piece of checked baggage up to 15 kg per flight.

SpiceJet does not provide through check-in for onward connections, either with SpiceJet or other airlines. As a point-to-point service, passengers must check in separately for any connecting flights. SpiceJet is not responsible for any missed connections or inconveniences related to other carriers. Passengers are advised to ensure a sufficient time gap between flights when changing airplanes or carriers at stopovers.
